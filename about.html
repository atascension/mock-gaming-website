Origin

Our Story

The Retro Gaming Archive started as a personal obsession — stacks of cartridges, shelves of consoles, and a nagging feeling that these machines deserved more than a garage sale. What began as one collector’s documentation project grew into something bigger: a dedicated archive built to preserve the stories, systems, and cultural moments that defined the first 30 years of gaming.

We believe that video game history is human history — shaped by the same creativity, competition, and passion that drives every great art form. And like all great art forms, it deserves to be recorded properly.

Purpose

The Mission

Our mission is simple: preserve and celebrate retro gaming history so that future generations can understand where this medium came from — and why it matters. Too many consoles end up in landfills. Too many stories go untold. Too many collectors have no platform to share what they’ve built.

The Archive exists to change that. We document, we interview, we film, and we publish — because the history of gaming is too important to let fade away.

The Series

The Show

Beyond the archive itself, we run an ongoing video series where we visit real collectors in their homes, workshops, and gaming rooms to document their collections on camera. Each episode is a journey into a living piece of gaming history — the stories behind the carts, the hunt for rare finds, and the passion that keeps the hobby alive.

Being featured means your collection gets a permanent home in the archive. A professionally filmed episode, a written feature, and a place in the record for collectors and gaming historians to find for years to come.
